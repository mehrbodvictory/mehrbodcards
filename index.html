How to play

Forge four elements. Break every board.

Every unit belongs to one of four tiers. Higher tiers hit harder, survive longer, and carry more chip slots — but there are far fewer of them in your deck.

Draft, then place.

You start with 12 units, 4 spells, and 2 chips. Each round you draw back up to 3 units in hand. During Placement, drag hand cards onto your 6 board slots — you're never forced to fill every slot.

Blue+Green=Red, Blue+Red or Green+Green=Orange. If the sum doesn't land exactly on a real tier (Green+Red=5) the merge is blocked — and Orange can't merge with anything, since there's nothing higher.

⚠ Get 4+ Blues on your board and everything else locks out — you must merge a Blue onto another Blue before you can place, cast, defend, attack, or ready up.

Blue is (almost) infinite.

Whenever one of your Blue cards is fused away or dies in combat, you get a fresh Blue back — as long as you still have at least one non-Blue card somewhere (deck, hand, or board).

The instant you're reduced to nothing but Blues, that stops for good: no more free Blues on merge or death, and your remaining Blues also lose the ability to defend. Spend your non-Blue cards wisely.

Defending is self-only.

A defending card blocks all damage aimed at itself and becomes invincible for the round — but it forfeits its own attack, and it never protects anything but itself.

Attacks land all at once.

There are no turns during the Attack phase — both players assign targets, then everyone readies up, and every attack resolves simultaneously. Deaths are applied together, and on-death abilities fire right after.

If an attacker dies before resolution (say, from a spell) its swing doesn't just vanish — it's queued and lands the instant next round begins.

Watch the battlefield: every hit now draws a streak from attacker to target, and every kill is labeled with exactly what caused it — a card's own attack, a spell, or a triggered ability — so it's never a mystery why something died.

Spells, chips, and winning.

Spells (4 per deck) hit a target immediately: damage, healing, or refreshing a card's spent defense charges. Chips (2 per deck) permanently boost a card's damage or HP, limited by its chip slots (higher tiers hold more).

Win condition: wipe out the opponent's units, hand, and deck completely. If both sides hit zero in the same instant, whoever has more unused spells + chips wins.
