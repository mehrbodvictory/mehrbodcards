How to play

Draft. Each player has 12 unit cards, 4 spells and 2 chips. Units come in four tiers by color: Blue → Green → Red → Orange. Stats scale with tier: HP, DMG and chip slots all increase.

Merge. During placement, combine two of your own board cards — their tier values add together (capped at Orange). Merging never happens mid-attack. If you ever have 4+ Blue cards on your board, a merge is forced automatically.

Defend. Blue can defend unlimited times, Green twice, Red once, Orange never. A defending card blocks all damage aimed at itself and becomes invincible that round — but it cannot attack. It only defends itself, never another card. Once you have no non-Blue units left anywhere, your Blues lose the ability to defend.

Attack. Both players assign targets at the same time — no turns. Damage resolves simultaneously, then deaths are applied, then on-death abilities fire immediately. If a card was set to attack but died first (say, from a spell), its attack is queued and lands at the start of the next placement round instead.

Spells & chips can be played at any time, in any phase, and hit their target immediately.

Win. Wipe out the opponent's units, hand and deck. If both sides hit zero at once, whoever has more unused spells+chips wins.
